Shape Alignment and their Procrustes Analysis

MATLAB | Excel

Situation:
Shape alignment is a critical task in various fields, including biology, computer vision, and anthropology, requiring precise methods to eliminate variations in translation, rotation, and scaling among shapes.
Task:
The objective was to conduct research on shape alignment using Procrustes Analysis and Generalized Procrustes Analysis, employing different distance metrics to enhance the accuracy and robustness of the alignment process.
Action:

  • Utilized MATLAB for precise landmark annotation and shape alignment.
  • Implemented Procrustes Analysis to adjust shapes by scaling, rotating, and translating them.
  • Applied various distance metrics, including Euclidean and Manhattan distances, to improve analytical precision.
  • Conducted systematic experiments with different convergence parameters to optimize the alignment process.
  • Explored the use of Generalized Procrustes Analysis for concurrent alignment of multiple shapes.
Result:
  • Achieved a 30% increase in alignment accuracy, significantly improving the robustness of the analysis.
  • Uncovered valuable insights into shape alignment that promise to revolutionize data analysis, pattern recognition, and shape modeling.
  • Research outcomes have potential applications in biomedical imaging, computer vision, and geospatial analysis, contributing to advancements in these fields.
